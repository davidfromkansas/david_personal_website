David Lie-Tjauw
Resume Press

David most recently worked at eBay as a Senior Product Manager where he launched "Explore" -- an endless algorithmic feed experience that allows shoppers to easily browse relevant item recommendations that are personalized based on who you are and what you like.

Previously at Twitter, David was the first PM for the Twitter Shopping team where he quickly launched new, monetizable shopping experiences like Twitter Shops, Live Shopping, Shoppable Profiles, and Product Drops. He also bootstrapped Twitter's merchant base from 0 to 10,000 in <1 year.

David initially joined Twitter as a PM on the Growth Acquisition team, driving a +XX million increase in DAUs by leading efforts to drive more logins and signups while inventing ways to boost Twitter content virality across the internet.

Originally, David started his PM journey in Google’s Associate Product Manager program on the Chromebook Team, launching “What’s New" which highlights major ChromeOS updates to millions of Chromebook users and is the first thing you see whenever your Chrome Browser updates (try in Chrome).

David has a B.S. in Computer Science from Washington University in Saint Louis. While in college, he worked as a software engineer at:

  • Google, where he shipped a visualization tool that allows customers to easily diagnose problems and monitor activity among entire fleets of IoT devices.
  • NASA, where he built a Bluetooth-based tracking system for NASA assets, prototyping bike and package tracking on the JSC campus with potential space applications. His work earned $200,000 in funding from NASA Director Ellen Ochoa and an award for individuals who support the space program.
